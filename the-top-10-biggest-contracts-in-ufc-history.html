The top 10 Biggest Contracts in UFC History

In the world of mixed martial arts (MMA), the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) stands as the premier organization. Over the years, the UFC has signed several lucrative contracts with its fighters, solidifying their positions at the top of the sport. This article explores the top 10 biggest contracts in UFC history, highlighting the fighters who have secured massive deals.

Conor McGregor - $100 million

Conor McGregor, the brash Irishman known for his showmanship and knockout power, tops the list with a reported $100 million contract. McGregor's charismatic personality and ability to draw massive pay-per-view numbers have made him one of the highest-paid athletes in combat sports.

Khabib Nurmagomedov - $50 million

Khabib Nurmagomedov, the undefeated Russian champion, secured a $50 million contract before retiring in 2020. Nurmagomedov's dominant performances inside the octagon and his large fan base in Russia and beyond made him a valuable asset for the UFC.

Georges St-Pierre - $40 million

Georges St-Pierre, a Canadian MMA legend, signed a $40 million contract during his time with the UFC. St-Pierre's incredible skills and popularity in both Canada and the United States contributed to his lucrative deal.

Jon Jones - $30 million

Jon Jones, widely regarded as one of the greatest fighters in MMA history, has had a tumultuous relationship with the UFC. Despite the controversies, Jones secured a $30 million contract, reflecting his immense talent and drawing power.

Brock Lesnar - $28 million

Brock Lesnar, a former professional wrestler turned MMA fighter, made a huge impact during his time with the UFC. Lesnar's crossover appeal and massive fan following from his WWE days led to a $28 million contract.

Anderson Silva - $20 million

Anderson Silva, a Brazilian middleweight legend, amassed a remarkable record during his tenure with the UFC. Silva's impressive striking and charismatic personality earned him a $20 million contract.

Ronda Rousey - $15 million

Ronda Rousey, a trailblazing female fighter, made a significant impact on women's MMA during her time with the UFC. Rousey's marketability and dominance in the Octagon landed her a $15 million contract.

Nate Diaz - $14 million

Nate Diaz, a fan-favorite and one of the most outspoken fighters in the UFC, secured a $14 million contract. Diaz's exciting fighting style and ability to captivate audiences with his post-fight interviews made him a valuable asset for the promotion.

Daniel Cormier - $12 million

Daniel Cormier, a two-division champion and highly respected figure in the sport, earned a $12 million contract during his time with the UFC. Cormier's skill set and professionalism inside and outside the Octagon contributed to his lucrative deal.

Jose Aldo - $11 million

Jose Aldo, a Brazilian featherweight legend, had a long and successful career in the UFC. Aldo's explosive fighting style and popularity in Brazil resulted in an $11 million contract.

The UFC has consistently rewarded its top fighters with lucrative contracts, reflecting their contributions to the sport and their drawing power. Conor McGregor, Khabib Nurmagomedov, Georges St-Pierre, Jon Jones, and Brock Lesnar are among the fighters who have secured the biggest contracts in UFC history. These contracts not only demonstrate the financial success of the fighters but also the continued growth and popularity of the sport itself.
