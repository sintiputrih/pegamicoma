Career

Howie Dorough Howie started singing at the age of three. Who would have thought he performed at the tender age of six or seven? He participated in the musicals at his elementary school as well. He received professional instruction in singing, dancing, and acting in a performing arts academy. He participated in Talent and TV programs while being an active school choir member. He gradually gained notoriety by appearing in movies, TV shows, and Disney World commercials.

He experienced several failures of his own, but he never gave up. Along with singing and acting, he worked at several odd jobs to support himself in the real world. In addition to being selected Most Talented, he was ranked in the top 10 of his class. The University of Central Florida then awarded him an Associate of Arts degree.

After high school, he joined Backstreet Boys in 1993. The trio performed at high schools and shopping centers before traveling to Europe with Jive Records. They sold an astounding 8 million copies of their album, which peaked in the top ten. They issued a record in the USA following the release of their second album, “Backstreet Back” in Europe. With almost 14 million copies sold, it peaked at No. 4 on the Billboard 200.

One of their best-selling albums was “Millenium” from 1999. 1.59 million copies of their next album, “Black and Blue,” were sold. After such a remarkable performance, they were immortalized. They later released further albums, including “Never Gone,” “Unbreakable,” “This is us,” “In a world like this,” and “DNA” after a brief hiatus. These albums broke sales records. They are one of the best-selling musicians right now, with 135 million albums sold worldwide.

He performed as a solo artist and released his debut album, “Back to me,” in 2011. His second album, “Which one am I,” was released in 2019 with little success.

He has also contributed to a number of TV shows. He has worked hard to build real estate and has held prestigious positions in a number of organizations, including his own Dorough Lupus Foundation. He took part in a musical that was partially based on his life in the beginning of this year.
