2 Chainz’s Breakthrough and Success as a Solo Artist

In 2011, the artist previously known as Tity Boi opted to change his moniker to 2 Chainz, believing that this name was more “family-friendly” and suited his emerging solo career.

This transition coincided with the release of his inaugural solo project, a mixtape titled “T.R.U. REALigion.”

Armed with his new identity as a solo artist, 2 Chainz embarked on collaborations with renowned musicians like Kanye West and Nicki Minaj.

By 2012, 2 Chainz was poised to unveil his debut studio album as a solo act.

It was titled “Based on a T.R.U. Story.”

The album featured a lead single, “No Lie,” featuring Drake. Remarkably, it debuted at the top spot on the Billboard Top 200 chart, selling 147,000 units within a week.

While reviews of the album were mixed, its achievements were undeniable. It opened doors for international tours with sold-out audiences, earning multiple award nominations, including three Grammys.

Additionally, 2 Chainz clinched several BET Hip Hop Awards and secured lucrative endorsement deals with various brands.
