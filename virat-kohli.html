Childhood & Early Life

Virat Kohli was born in Delhi, India, on November 5, 1988, to Prem Kohli and Saroj Kohli. His father was a criminal defense attorney, and his mother is a housewife.

He has two siblings: Vikash, his elder brother, and Bhavna, his elder sister.
He first became interested in cricket as a child. He would pick up a cricket bat at the age of three and ask his father to bowl to him. Vishal Bharti Public School was where he received his early education.

Virat’s interest in cricket grew, and when he was nine, his father enrolled him at the West Delhi Cricket Academy. He trained at the Rajkumar Sharma Academy and also competed at the Sumit Dogra Academy.

Career

He played for the Delhi Under-15 team in 2002 and was the leading run-getter in the Polly Umrigar Trophy tournament that year. He was named captain of the Polly Umrigar Trophy team in 2003-04.

He was named to the Delhi Under-17 team for the Vijay Merchant Trophy in 2003-04. He scored 470 runs in four matches, averaging 117.50 and hitting two hundreds. He repeated his outstanding performance the following season, helping Delhi win the 2004-05 Vijay Merchant Trophy.

His consistent performances earned him a spot in the India Under-19 squad during its 2006 tour of England. In the three-match ODI series, which India Under-19 won, he averaged 105. Later that year, he also impressed against Pakistan’s U-19 cricket team.

Kohli’s father died in 2006, when he was only 18 years old. His father had been his greatest supporter, and his untimely death had thrown the family into financial ruin. Kohli began to take the game more seriously now that he had more responsibilities on his young shoulders.

He led India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup, which was held in Malaysia. Following that, he was signed on a youth contract by Royal Challengers Bangalore of the Indian Premier League for $30,000 USD.

He was named to the Indian One-Day International squad for the 2008 tour of Sri Lanka. He batted as a makeshift opener throughout the tour because both regular openers Sachin Tendulkar and Virender Sehwag were injured. India eventually won the series 3-2, marking India’s first ODI series victory in Sri Lanka.

Throughout 2009, he maintained a consistent level of performance. He was named man of the match for the first time after scoring an unbeaten 79 against the West Indies in a group match at the 2009 ICC Champions Trophy. Later that year, he played in a home ODI series against Sri Lanka, scoring his first ODI century (107 off 111 balls) in the series’ fourth match.

He maintained his good form, earning him a spot in the 2011 World Cup squad. Kohli appeared in every match of the tournament and scored an unbeaten century against Bangladesh in the first game, becoming the first Indian batsman to score a century on World Cup debut. India advanced to the finals, and Kohli scored an important 35 runs against Sri Lanka. India went on to win the World Cup by six wickets for the first time since 1983.

During India’s tour of the West Indies in June and July 2011, the team was largely inexperienced, with Kohli one of three uncapped players in the Test squad. In the first match of the series, he made his Test debut at Kingston. India won the Test series 1-0, but Kohli struggled against fast bowling. In the series, he scored 76 runs in five innings and was subsequently dropped from the Test squad.

He was called up to the Test squad for India’s four-match series in England in July and August 2011, replacing the injured Yuvraj Singh. He did not play in any Test matches during the series, but he was reasonably successful in the ODIs.
During India’s tour of Australia in December 2011, he made his Test debut. India lost the Test series 4-0, but Virat Kohli was the series’ leading run-scorer. He scored his first Test century in the first innings of the fourth and final match at Adelaide, scoring 116 runs.

Kohli was named vice-captain for the Asia Cup in Bangladesh in 2012. He broke West Indies’ Brian Lara’s long-standing record of 156 in the final group stage match against Pakistan, scoring 183 while chasing—the highest individual score against Pakistan in ODIs. Despite his outstanding performances, India was unable to advance to the tournament’s final.

Throughout 2013, he continued to have a string of successful stints. He performed admirably in the seven-match One-Day International series against Australia, scoring the fastest century by an Indian in ODIs in the second match at Jaipur, hitting a century off 52 balls. His 100-not-out innings aided India’s pursuit of the 360-point target.
Kohli is a skilled test player who is better known for his blazing ODI performances. He was named captain of the Indian test squad in 2014. During the same year’s Australian tour, he scored 692 runs in four Tests, the most by any Indian batsman in an Australian Test series.

In 2015, he experienced a brief slump in his career due to a lack of big scores. However, he quickly regained his form and became the world’s fastest batsman to reach 1,000 runs in T20I cricket during South Africa’s tour of India later that year.

Kohli was in excellent form in 2016. His outstanding batting performances helped India advance to the semifinals of the 2016 T20 World Cup, which was held in India.

Virat scored 2818 runs in international matches in 2017, the third-highest total in a calendar year and the highest by an Indian cricketer. The ICC named him captain of the World Test XI and ODI XI for 2017.

Kohli scored 593 runs in the 5-match test series against England in 2018, the third highest total by an Indian batsman in a losing test series. His batting was praised as one of his best by the British media. According to The Guardian, Kohli’s batting display was “one of the greatest batting displays in a losing cause.” That same year, Kohli became the fastest player to score 10,000 ODI runs, doing so in 205 innings.

Kohli resigned as India’s T20I captain in September 2021, and Rohit Sharma took over as India’s ODI captain in December 2021. Kohli stepped down as India’s Test captain on January 15, 2022, following a 2-1 loss to South Africa.

Philanthropic Works

In 2013, Virat Kohli established the ‘Virat Kohli Foundation’ (VKF) to assist underprivileged children. The foundation also holds fundraising events to benefit charitable causes.

Achievements and Awards

He was named ICC One-Day International Player of the Year in 2012, as well as BCCI International Cricketer of the Year in 2011-12 and 2014-15.

In 2013, he was awarded the Arjuna Award for his achievements in international cricket.
