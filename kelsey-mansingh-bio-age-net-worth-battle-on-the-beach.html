Kelsey Mansingh On HGTV’s Kelsey Mansingh

Six new episodes of HGTV’s Battle on the Beach debuted on Sunday, June 4, at 9 p.m. ET/PT. The big news is that this round’s judges will be Renovation Island’s Sarah and Bryan Baeumler. If you haven’t seen Battle on the Beach’s first two seasons, the basic premise is as follows: In Gulf Shores, Alabama, three teams of aspiring home renovators compete to add the most significant value to a trio of two-bedroom, two-bath seaside homes.

In addition to their instructors, HGTV design professionals Taniya Nayak (Build it Forward), Ty Pennington (Rock the Block), and Alison Victoria (Windy City Rehab), have a $90,000 budget to work with. Who wins the $50,000 cash is chosen by the judges and a panel of regional real estate professionals.

Season 3 of Battle on the Beach will feature more than just new judges. This time, one of the properties will have an additional 200 square feet rather than three identical homes (whether that’s a good or negative thing is yet to be determined). Prior to the teams beginning their kitchen and dining room makeovers in episode one, a kick-off competition will determine who receives the larger home.

The contestant competing for the grand prize are married couple Ryan and Kelsey Mansingh of Ohio. Major home modifications don’t frighten the young couple. They were unable to afford upgrades when they constructed a two-story home in Ryan’s hometown of Orlando, Florida, six years ago. So they used FaceTime to FaceTime with Kelsey’s brother, who headed her family’s Helen Scott Custom Builders in Barberton, Ohio, to learn how to create them by watching YouTube videos.

The others competing on the show are also friends Ashley Basnight and Steve Lewis of Oklahoma, and friends Ben Argall and Pete Meldrum of Michigan.

What Is Kelsey Mansingh Maiden Name?

The maiden name of Kelsey Mansingh is Strayer. She is the daughter of Randal Strayer and Linda Strayer. Linda who is currently 70 years old, was born in February 1953. Randal turned 66 years old in April 1957. Both of her parents are residing in New Franklin, Ohio.

Kelsey lost her grandfather James W. “Jim” Strayer died at the age of 85 in 2013. He was a local artist and retired advertising executive. James was married to his wife and high school sweetheart, Eleanor “Norie” Strayer. They were married for 52 years before his wife’s death.

Jim graduated from Fenton High School, Fenton, Mich., in 1946 prior to serving in the U.S. Navy. After his military service, he earned his BA at the Meinzinger School of Art in Detroit. Prior to moving to Hilton Head Island, Jim displayed his paintings in numerous art exhibits in Michigan and Ohio also doing commission work. On Hilton Head, he was a regular exhibitor at the Art Gallery for more than twenty-five years. Jim’s art is included in many private and commercial art collections from Connecticut to California.

Jim was also a long-time devotee of “jazz” music, dating back to his high school days. He was a musician (drummer).

Kelsey has at least two siblings: a sister named Ashley Strayer who is 36 years old. She also has a 39-year-old brother named Scott Strayer who is the president of Helen Scott Builders. He is married to his wife Kristen Strayer.

Her family has worked for generations in homebuilding. Her grandparents, Helen and Verne Scott, are the founders of Helen Scott Builders, the Barberton-based home-building company that got its start in 1968.
