Gisele Bündchen children-Brazilian fashion model, Gisele Bündchen was born on July 20th, 1980 in Horizontina, Rio Grande do Sul in Brazil.

Born to Vânia, a retired bank teller, and writer and sociologist Valdir Bündchen, Bündchen is a sixth-generation German Brazilian. Walter Bündchen, her grandpa, once presided over Horizontina.

Raquel, Graziela, Gabriela, Rafaela, and her fraternal twin, Patrcia, were his five sisters. It was a Catholic family. Bündchen learned German in school and her parents also speak the language, but she no longer uses it.

Portuguese, English, Italian, Spanish, and a little bit of French are among Bündchen’s other major languages. Although Bündchen wanted to play volleyball, her mother put her, along with her sisters Patrcia and Gabriela, in a modeling training in 1993 to instill in them self-assurance and proper posture. 

She was found by Elite Model Management at a mall after the course when the ladies were given a trip to Curitiba, So Paulo, and Rio de Janeiro as a reward.

At the time known as Look of the Year, the Elite Model Look nationwide competition saw Bündchen 

finish in second place. Bündchen relocated to So Paulo in 1995 to begin her modeling career. She made her debut in 1996 during New York Fashion Week.

Before being chosen to appear in an Alexander McQueen runway show in 1998, Bündchen was denied 42 times in London in 1997. [28] For advertisements in 1998, Bündchen appeared in front of the camera for Missoni, Chloé, Dolce & Gabbana, Valentino, Gianfranco Ferré, Ralph Lauren, and Versace. She was featured on the covers of Vogue Paris, her first appearance in a British Vogue issue, and i-D, which featured “A Girl Called Gisele” as its cover story.

Bündchen had an appearance on 37 worldwide Vogue covers in 2000, three of which were for the American edition. In January 2000, both the American and British editions of Vogue featured Bündchen on their covers. She served as the show opener for Marc Jacobs, Michael Kors, Dolce & Gabbana, Christian Dior, and Valentino during spring 2000 fashion week in New York, Milan, and Paris.

Together with Daria Werbowy, Natalia Vodianova, Isabeli Fontana, Karolina Kurkova, Liya Kebede, Hana Soukupova, Gemma Ward, and Karen Elson, she appeared on a group U.S. Vogue cover in 2004. In addition to the Pirelli Calendar, Bündchen was featured on the covers of W, Allure, GQ, Forbes, Marie Claire, Time, Vanity Fair, and Allure.

In May 2009, The Independent dubbed Bündchen the biggest star in fashion history. Bündchen made her 11th appearance on the American Vogue cover in April 2010. The 2010 Balenciaga show finale featured a surprise visit from Bündchen. 2011 saw Bündchen listed as one of the Top Accomplished Women Entertainers by CEOWorld Magazine.

More than any other model or celebrity, Bündchen featured two covers of the Vogue Shape issue by 2010. On the 2011 FHM Australia list of the 100 sexiest women in the world, she came in at number 45. Bündchen was ranked No. 25 among the 100 sexiest women of all time by Men’s Health in 2011.

Bündchen appeared in three spring 2012 advertising campaigns for Versace, Givenchy, and Salvatore Ferragamo. In 2012, she was chosen to represent Banco do Brasil’s first international advertising campaign.

Ipanema sandals by Gisele Bündchen, which sold roughly 25 million pairs annually in 2013, were responsible for more than half of Grendene’s about $250 million in annual exports.

Bündchen secured a contract with Under Armour in 2014 that apparently compares favorably to the endorsement payouts received by professional athletes and outpaces her prior deal with Victoria’s Secret.

Gisele published her book Lessons: My Path to a Meaningful Life in 2018, and it became a New York Times bestseller and Brazil’s best-selling non-fiction book for more than six months.
