Tom Pelphrey Partner: Meet Kaley Cuoco

Tom Pelphrey partner-American actor, Thomas Pelphrey was born on July 28th, 1982 in Howell, New Jersey in the United States of America.

Pelphrey earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from Rutgers University’s Mason Gross School of the Arts in 2004 after graduating from Howell High School in 2000.

He has portrayed Jonathan Randall and Mick Dante on the CBS television programs Guiding Light and As the World Turns, respectively, as well as Kurt Bunker in the Cinemax television series Banshee, Ward Meachum in the Netflix original series Iron Fist, Ben Davis in the Netflix original series Ozark, and Perry Abbott in the Prime Video original series Outer Range. 

He also had a supporting role in David Fincher’s film Mank and played Jason Derek Brown, the lead character, in the true crime film.

Tom Pelphrey career

On the CBS serial series Guiding Light, Pelphrey played Jonathan Randall, the son of Reva Shayne and her deceased ex-husband, Richard Winslow.

In April of the following year, 2006, he received the Daytime Emmy Award for “Outstanding Younger Actor,” for which he was nominated in 2005.

In 2007, he was nominated in the same category for the third time in a row, but Bryton McClure won. In 2008, Pelphrey received his second Daytime Emmy for “Outstanding Younger Actor.”

In October 2009, Pelphrey agreed to a deal to portray “Mick” on As the World Turns. His stint came to an end in February 2010 when his villainous persona was imprisoned. For his performance as Mick Dante, Pelphrey received an Emmy nomination for Best Supporting Actor.

He made an appearance in the Numb3rs episode “Pandora’s Box” on March 30, 2007. He made an appearance in an episode of “Ghost Whisperer” on October 17, 2008, then in an episode of Law & Order: Special Victims Unit the following year.

For Cinemax’s action thriller Banshee in 2015, Pelphrey was cast in a recurring role as Kurt Bunker, a former Nazi who is attempting to reintegrate into society.

He played Ward Meachum in Iron Fist in 2017, and on the Netflix crime thriller Ozark’s third season, which premiered on March 27, 2020, he played Ben Davis.

Fans and journalists alike praised his performance in the latter, and the absence of an Emmy nomination was seen as a slight.

Pelphrey was nevertheless nominated for the Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Drama Series, the Satellite Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Series, Miniseries, or Television Film, and the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series (shared with the cast).

Pelphrey played Ben Davis again in a guest appearance on Ozark in the show’s final season (2022), for which he was nominated for an Emmy in the category of Outstanding Guest Actor in a Drama Series.

He most recently played Perry Abbott in the Amazon Prime Video series Outer Range, and he’ll play Don Crowder in the forthcoming HBO Max miniseries Love and Death.

Who is Tom Pelphrey’s partner?

Pelphrey is not married but in a relationship with actress, Kaley Cuoco. They started dating in 2022 and made their first public appearance as a couple at a Hollywood Walk of Fame ceremony in May 2022.

Who is Kaley Cuoco?

Kaley Cuoco is an American actress. After a series of supporting film and television roles in the late 1990s, she landed her breakthrough role as Bridget Hennessy on the ABC sitcom 8 Simple Rules (2002–2005).
