Early Life

Tony Romo was born on 21 April 1980 and he is currently 43 years old. He was born in San Diego, California when his father was stationed at the San Diego U.S. Naval Base.

Therefore, he is also famous as a “Navy brat”. His father is Ramiro Romo Jr. and his mother is Joan Jakubowski.

Likewise, he has two siblings namely Danielle and Jossalyn. Further, his full name is Antonio Ramiro Romo.

After the family returned to Burlington, Wisconsin, his father worked as a carpenter and construction worker while his mother was a grocery store clerk.

This personality’s paternal grandfather is an immigrant from Múzquiz, Coahuila, Mexico.

His grandfather had then settled in San Antonio, Texas as an adolescent. From his mother’s side, this personality is of German and Polish ancestry.

Moreover, he was also a starter on the school’s varsity basketball team as well as played golf and tennis.

Career and Professional Life

Professionally, Tony is a former American footballer. He used to play as a quarterback for the National Football League (NFL) team Dallas Cowboys. He played with this team for 14 seasons. At present, Romo is an analyst.

This personality played college football at Eastern Illinois. At this institution, Romo made an Ohio Valley Conference championship appearance in the year 2001.

Additionally, he won the Walter Payton Award the very next year. Then in the year 2003, this player signed with the Cowboys as an undrafted free agent.

Moreover, Romo started off his career in a backup role. As such, this player served as the Cowboys’ primary starter from the year 2006 to 2015.

Further, this player led his team to four postseason appearances during his tenure. He also received Pro Bowl honors amid each playoff run.
