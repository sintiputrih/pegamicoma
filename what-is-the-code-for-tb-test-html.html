What exactly is a positive PPD?

The PPD — pure protein derivative — skin test is the most often used to screen for tuberculosis. Positive PPD results indicate that you have been exposed to a person who has TB and that you have been infected with the bacteria (Mycobacterium tuberculosis) that causes the illness.

What exactly is latent tuberculosis infection?

Infection with Mycobacterium tuberculosis (LTBI) is characterised by a sustained immunological response to stimulation by Mycobacterium tuberculosis antigens in the absence of clinically evident active tuberculosis. Someone has latent tuberculosis if they are infected with the TB bacteria but do not show indications of current TB illness and do not seem to be unwell as a result of the infection.

What exactly is tuberculosis

A: Tuberculosis (TB) is a disease caused by bacteria (Mycobacterium tuberculosis) that affects the lungs the majority of time. Tuberculosis is a disease that may be treated and prevented. What exactly is a QW modifier?

Modifier QW is defined as a test that has been waived under the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendment (CLIA). The modifier is used to indicate tests that have been waived, and it must be included in the first modifier box. Tests that have been waived by the CLIA and need the QW modifier are referred to as streamlined analysis tests.
