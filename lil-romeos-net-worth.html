Music

Lil Romeo’s career in music began at a young age, when he was signed to a No Limit Records subsidiary label named Soulja Music Entertainment at the age of five.

After writing a short rap for his father, Master P, who was the owner of the label No Limit Records at the time, he was signed to Soulja Music Entertainment.

Lil Romeo’s debut album, “Lil’ Romeo,” was released in 2001, just 7 years after he was signed, under the No Limit Records label, and featured the hit single “My Baby.”

The album “Lil’ Romeo” was a tremendous hit, selling over 200,000 copies in its first week and reaching number 6 on the Billboard 200.

It was also certified Gold by the Recording Industry Association of America.

Lil Romeo’s second album, “Game Time,” was released in 2002 and was a commercial success.

Furthermore, it reached number 10 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums list and number 33 on the Billboard 200.

The album “Game Time” generated more sales than his debut album, although not being as successful in the charts.

In the United States, it sold roughly 2,500,000 copies.

“Romeoland” Album

The album “Romeoland,” like his second, was not as successful on the charts as his first, peaking at #70 on the Billboard 200.

Nonetheless, it was a huge success, selling over 250,000 copies in the first week of its release in the United States.

After the publication of “Romeoland,” his final album under the name “Lil Romeo,” he went under the moniker “Romeo.”

Later, in 2005, Romeo collaborated with his father to launch a new record label called Guttar Music Entertainment.

In 2007, Romeo and his father founded a non-explicit record label called “Take A Stand Records,” which they named after his father.

In September 2007, they established a group called “Miller Boyz” and released the album “Hip Hop History.”

Romeo, on the other hand, continued to focus on his solo career, releasing multiple hit albums, including “Gumbo Station” in 2008 and the album compilation “Get Low LP” in March 2009.

In late 2008, Romeo founded Next Generation Entertainment, a record label with which he collaborated with the group College Boyys.

In May 2010, the duo published their debut album, “Spring Break,” which was a commercial success, selling over 30,000 copies in its first week.
