Paige Spiranac Pours Her Heart Out Over Tiger Woods' Recent Struggle

According to Golfweek, Tiger Woods was spotted in Tulsa, Oklahoma competing at the PGA Championship at Southern Hills Country Club. And, let's just say, no one was impressed by his performance. In fact, many of his peers were saddened by how he was playing and could visibly see the pain he was in.

"It's hard watching Tiger be in so much pain," golf instructor Paige Spiranac wrote on Twitter. "The fight he has to get through this round is incredible." Sports broadcaster Gary Lineker empathized with Woods, too, but also felt bad watching him go out like that. "Difficult to watch Tiger Woods struggle and hobble around the golf course," Lineker shared on Twitter. "It's inspiring to witness his incredible mentality. He never, ever gives up." 

Woods ultimately withdrew from the PGA Championship right before the weekend's final round, per Golf Digest. According to his agent, the "pain and discomfort in his right foot" is what prompted his team to make the decision. "I just didn't play well," he said. Woods was gearing up to come in last place and even struggled in the beginning of the first two rounds. "I didn't hit the ball very well, and got off to not the start I needed to get off to," he added. It's unclear right now what Woods' future career plans are, but either way, he will always be a legend.
